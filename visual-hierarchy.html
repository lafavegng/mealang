Guiding the Eye: Principles of Visual Hierarchy

MEA Negative-Space Logo: M as mountain of potential; hover reveals E (energy bridge) and A (alliance peak).

Visual hierarchy is the arrangement of elements in a way that implies importance. By strategically using size, color, and placement, designers guide the viewer's eye through a composition in a specific, intended order. This reduces cognitive load, allowing information to be absorbed quickly and intuitively.

Core Tools

Size & Scale

The easiest way to signal importance. Larger objects command attention first.

Primary Goal: Direct Focus

Impact: Highest Visibility

Risk: Can overwhelm if too large

Color & Contrast

Directs attention to specific actions. Bright colors on muted backgrounds pop.

Primary Goal: Signal Action

Impact: High Emotional Response

Risk: Too many colors dilute focus

Typography

Organizes text into a readable path. Weights and styles create a story.

Primary Goal: Logical Reading

Impact: Increases Scannability

Risk: Inconsistent styles confuse users

Spatial Logic

The Breathing Room

Adequate white space reduces cognitive load and signals a 'premium' or refined brand feel.

Grouped Context

Use proximity to group related buttons or navigation links so users understand they belong together.

Visual Alignment

Aligning text and images along a common axis creates an invisible path for the eyes to follow.

Eye Patterns

Predicting Human Scanning: Humans scan content in predictable patterns based on their reading habits. Designing with these patterns in mind ensures key info isn't missed.

Common Scanning Patterns

The F-Pattern

Common for text-heavy pages like blogs. Users read horizontally across the top, then vertically down the left side.

Best For: Articles, Blogs

Key Real Estate: Top bar, Left margin

Behavior: Skimming for keywords

The Z-Pattern

Ideal for visual-heavy pages or landing pages. The eye moves in a 'Z' shape from top-left to bottom-right.

Best For: Landing Pages

Key Real Estate: Hero banner, Call to Action

Behavior: Quick scanning for value
